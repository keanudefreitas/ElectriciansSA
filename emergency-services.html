What Constitutes an Electrical Emergency?

  • Complete power loss: No electricity to your home or business
  • Burning smells: Potential electrical fire hazard
  • Electrical shocks: Risk of injury from faulty wiring
  • Sparking outlets: Immediate danger
  • Tripping circuit breakers: Repeated issues causing power loss
  • Flickering lights: Indicating serious electrical problems
  • Exposed wiring: Safety hazard requiring immediate attention
  • Water and electricity contact: Flood damage to electrical systems

Emergency Safety Tips

While Waiting for Our Arrival:

  • Turn off the main power switch if safe to do so
  • Do not touch electrical outlets, switches, or wet areas
  • Keep children and pets away from the problem area
  • Do not attempt DIY electrical fixes - let professionals handle it
  • Stay away from sparking wires or burning equipment
  • If there's a fire, evacuate immediately and call emergency services

Types of Emergencies We Handle

Electrical Failures

  • Complete power outage in home or business
  • Individual circuit breaker failure
  • Distribution board (DB board) problems
  • Transformer issues

Safety Hazards

  • Electrical shocks or buzzing sounds
  • Burning smells or smoke from outlets
  • Exposed or damaged wiring
  • Water damage to electrical systems

System Failures

  • Repeated tripping of circuit breakers
  • Flickering or dimming lights throughout property
  • Dead outlets or switches
  • Overheated cables or equipment
